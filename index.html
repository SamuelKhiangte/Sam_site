About Me

Welcome! I am a researcher based at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc). My work lies at the intersection of statistical physics and deep learning. I focus on the theoretical modeling of soft matter, coarse-grained active dynamics, and smart agent collectives.

I actively leverage machine learning architectures—such as Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and Transformers—alongside JAX and TensorFlow to understand non-equilibrium systems and biological agents. Whether I am deriving cluster expansion equations or managing large-scale HPC cluster simulations, my goal is to uncover the underlying rules governing complex physical systems.

My research aims to build bridge frameworks where physical constraints are embedded directly into neural architectures to facilitate the modeling of active biological cell matrices, and dynamic cell-cell adhesion behaviors.
